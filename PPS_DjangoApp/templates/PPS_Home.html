About Post-Polio Syndrome

What is Post-Polio Syndrome?

Post-Polio Syndrome (PPS) is a secondary result of having had an acute paralytic viral poliomyelitis infection earlier in life. It appears to occur in between 25 and 60 percent (depending on which study is referenced) of Polio Survivors, and is characterized by symptoms of increasing weakness/loss of function in muscle groups previously affected by the acute infection, severe and/or sudden fatigue (often requiring unusually long recovery periods) following what used to be 'everyday' activities, and problems with deterioration of posture, balance, breathing, and/or swallowing.
